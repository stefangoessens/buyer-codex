# PayFit

Role in hierarchy: primary aesthetic reference.

## What Was Captured

- Hero typography and CTA pair
- Trust row and proof close
- Feature tabs and metric triptych
- Testimonial + FAQ + final CTA close
- Mobile stacked hero and collapsed navigation

## Key Observations

### Typography

- Display and body styles now read closer to SF Pro than to Inter.
- Display headings are semibold, tightly led, and visually calm.
- The right implementation move for buyer-codex is to approximate this with `Geist`, not to import Hosman's more branded Poppins voice into the whole system.

### Color and tone

- The page is white-first and restraint-first.
- Action emphasis comes from one confident blue instead of multiple accents.
- Secondary text and helper copy stay subdued, not washed out.

### CTA treatment

- Primary CTA is compact, rounded, and decisive.
- Secondary CTA stays visible but much quieter.
- Buttons are not oversized pills; they feel deliberate and operational.

### Trust close

- The page closes with testimonials, FAQ, and a final CTA rather than more feature marketing.
- This is the right pattern for buyer-codex trust sections.

## Adopt

- Typography tone and restraint
- Filled + quiet-secondary CTA pair
- FAQ + testimonial + final CTA close
- Low-amplitude motion and hover feedback only where it clarifies interaction

## Reject

- News/promotional ticker above navigation
- Long product-module catalog sections
- Payroll-specific proof patterns and HR iconography
